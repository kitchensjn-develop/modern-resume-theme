NASA Develop Intern - Jet Propulsion Laboratory

January 2020 - April 2020

Arizona State University's Mathematical and Theoretical Biology Institute

Summer Research Intern

June 2019 - July 2019

I worked as a research intern during an eight week summer term at Arizona State University. The first four weeks of the program included guest lectures on complex mathematical topics and their application to biological research. The last four weeks focused on the development of an original research project. I was the creator and lead for a team studying the Topography and Behavior Based Movement Modeling for Missing Hikers in Land-Wilderness Settings, including the development of cellular automata simulations in Python. We analyzed how certain terrain features generate natural bottlenecking effects. Knowledge of these effects could potentially aid in efficient search efforts across terrain that is less familiar to search and rescue teams.

University of Wisconsin - La Crosse

Summer Research Intern

May 2018 - August 2018

Paired with professors from UW - La Crosse and members of the United States Geological Survey, I used environmental DNA data to model population size of an invasive species (Petromyzon marinus) within the Great Lakes. A graduate student at the university had previously collected the data, which included sample extraction and quantitative PCR measurements. I developed models in R to analyze both copy number measurements and presence/absence observations. I found that environmental DNA was strongly correlated with population size in a very controlled setting, such as a pool, but had limited use beyond presence/absence detection when utilized in a natural setting due to the more complex system of compounding factors. Through this project, I gained experience in statistical programming.

Warren Wilson College's Genetics and Plant Physiology Laboratory

Research Assistant

August 2017 - December 2019

While at Warren Wilson College, I worked as a research assistant within Dr. Alisa Hove’s laboratory, where we studied human-impacted species, including the American pika (Ochotona princeps) and American ginseng (Panax quinquefolius). My capstone project was a genetic sex ratio analysis of American pika populations found in the Rocky Mountain Region. Working with samples collected by partners at University of Colorado Boulder, I created a protocol for DNA extraction and quantification from pika hair, tissue, and scat samples. Through work in the lab, I advanced my skills in PCR, gel electrophoresis, NMR spectroscopy, field sampling and phenological monitoring.

Virginia Institute for Psychiatric and Behavioral Genetics

Research Assistant

May 2014 - February 2016

I managed a database of over 6000 individuals who participated in the “Spit for Science Project” at Virginia Commonwealth University. I wrote programs in Python and SQLite to query the database and create summary statistics tables describing the available data. These tables included quality control measurements and were used to generate phenotypic risk scores for individuals based on their combination of single nucleotide polymorphisms. With the support of this work, I coauthored Webb et al. (2017). Molecular Genetic Influences on Normative and Problematic Alcohol Use in a Population-Based Sample of College Students. Frontiers in Genetics, 8, where we identified two DNA markers that were significantly associated with alcohol consumption behaviors.
